1. Executive Summary

What Are We Building?
Super Sauced is launching a mobile app that gives customers instant access to a curated recipe library built around the Super Sauced cube lineup, accessible directly via QR code on the packaging.

Problem Statement
For the business: Super Sauced sells exclusively through retail, which means there is no direct touchpoint with customers after purchase. The brand has no visibility into who is buying, how often, or how they are using the product.

For the customer: Most people do not know the full range of meals they can create with Super Sauced cubes. With the pain points of everyday meal decision fatigue and the pressure to get something on the table quickly and with minimal effort, users want guidance on effortless and nutritious meals. 

Proposed Solution
For customers: The app delivers a curated recipe library built around the Super Sauced lineup -- with step-by-step guides, GIFs, and save and share features that eliminate the "what do I make?" moment and simplify cooking into a more streamlined, low-effort experience.

For the business: The app creates a direct channel that retail cannot provide -- capturing first-party customer data, building a CRM, and giving Super Sauced an ongoing touchpoint to drive repeat purchase and brand loyalty. It also lays the foundation for direct selling as the product line expands to DTC-friendly SKUs, and opens the door to rewards programs and customer loyalty initiatives, all within the Shopify ecosystem.

Business Objective
Establish a direct-to-consumer digital channel that captures first-party customer data, drives repeat purchases through recipe engagement, and builds brand loyalty beyond the retail shelf -- while creating the infrastructure to support future DTC sales and customer loyalty programs.

Success Looks Like
25% app download conversion ratio, with measurable engagement in recipe saves and return visits.

5. Solution Overview
Super Sauced is launching a mobile app that gives customers who buy Super Sauced cubes in-store a clean, curated recipe experience built entirely around the product they already have. 
Unlike generic recipe websites cluttered with ads and life stories, the Super Sauced app gets straight to the recipe, with a distraction-free, step-by-step, GIF-guided cooking experience designed to make every meal effortless and every cube worth buying again.

Key User Journey
Customer buys product in-store, scans QR code on packaging, lands on a recipe page on the Super Sauced Shopify website, sees a prompt to download the app for more recipes and a richer experience, downloads the app, signs in with an existing Shopify account or creates a new one (syncs bidirectionally with Shopify), then explores the full recipe library, saves favorites, hearts recipes, follows step-by-step cooking guides, and shares with others.

Platform
    • iOS -- App Store (launch priority)
    • Android -- Google Play (to follow shortly after iOS launch)

Key Assumptions
    • Customers who scan the QR code are motivated enough to download the app if the web experience is compelling
    • Recipe content is the primary value that brings users back (MVP), not social or community features (those come later)

6. User Flows
Primary end-to-end journeys for MVP flow. Wireframes and screen-level design live in Figma -- link here once available. Flows describe sequence and logic only, not UI layout.

App Navigation Structure
The app uses a bottom tab navigation with four tabs: Recipes (main library, default tab), Saved (bookmarked recipes), Discover (Discovery Mode -- Phase 2), and Profile (account, preferences).

Flow 1 -- App Download (New User)
    • User scans QR code on product packaging with phone camera
    • Lands on recipe page at supersauced.com (Next.js) -- app download prompt visible above the fold
    • Taps download link -- routed to App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android)
    • Downloads and opens app
    • Sees sign-up screen -- chooses Apple Sign In, Google Sign In, or email magic link
    • If email: enters address, receives magic link, taps to authenticate
    • Prompted to enter phone number (should be verified)
    • Onboarding survey shown (each question individually skippable):
        ◦ Q1: Which Super Sauced cubes are you using? We’ll tailor your recipes based on your sauces! (multi-select, all SKUs listed, quantity selector – this will update Sauce Log in user’s Profile so they can track their inventory on hand)
        ◦ Q2: Any dietary preferences? You can set or update this later in your profile. (multi-select: vegan, high-protein, gluten-free, dairy-free, etc.)
        ◦ Q3: How did you hear about us? (single-select: In-Store, Instagram, Tik Tok, Linkedin, Word of Mouth, Other)
    • Taps Continue or Skip -- lands on Recipes home tab
    • Survey responses stored and used to personalize initial recipe feed and filter defaults

Flow 2 -- Recipe Discovery and Cooking (Core Loop)
    • Opens app (authenticated, session persists) -- lands on Recipes tab
    • Browses recipe library or uses search / filters (cube/SKU, cook time, dietary tag, difficulty)
    • Taps a recipe card -- views recipe detail
    • Reviews: hero image, cook time, difficulty, serving size, ingredients list, instructions and “More Recipes You'll Love” suggestions below
    • Adjusts serving size if needed -- ingredients and macros update in real time
    • Taps Start Cooking or toggles on “Guided Mode” to enter step by step video and text instructions flow
    • First step, clickable ingredient checklist shown based off serving size selected -- checks off each ingredient (or taps Next)
    • Step 1 shown: video plays, description visible, step timer visible if applicable
    • Taps Next to advance through each step; Back to return
    • Screen stays awake throughout -- no auto-lock
    • If step has a timer: taps countdown to start; taps to pause / reset
    • Complete final step -- prompted to adjust sauce quantity in Sauce Log, give one click feedback on recipe fit (heart or x), save recipe for next time. 

Flow 3 -- Returning User (Saved Recipes)
    • Opens app (authenticated) -- lands on Recipes tab
    • Taps Saved tab in bottom navigation
    • Browses saved recipe library or can filter for something specific (same filter functions as default Recipe tab)
    • Taps a saved recipe -- views recipe detail
    • Starts cooking via Guided Mode or uses Quick View Mode (default)

Flow 4 -- Engagement Actions (Heart, Save, Share)
    • From recipe card (library view): taps bookmark icon to save
    • From recipe detail (prompting user most likely has tried recipe): taps heart if they liked,  bookmark to save for future, or share button to share
    • Heart: count increments immediately; syncs to Supabase in background
    • Save: recipe added to Saved tab immediately; syncs to Supabase in background
    • Share: native share sheet opens -- user selects destination (iMessage, WhatsApp, Instagram, copy link, etc.)

Flow 5 -- Discovery Mode (Phase 2)
    • User taps Discover tab in bottom navigation
    • Optional filter panel shown -- set preferences: cube/SKU, dietary tag, cook time, difficulty (same as main Recipe tab)
    • First recipe card shown full-screen: hero image, recipe name, cook time, difficulty, key macros
    • Two interactions available on each card:
        ◦ Tap the card (Match) -- interested; opens the full recipe detail page
        ◦ Swipe left (Skip) -- not for me; next card shown immediately
    • Full recipe detail page has all standard features: Guided Mode, serving size adjuster, save, heart, share, More Recipes You'll Love
    • From recipe detail, user taps Back -- returns to Discover mode at exactly the same position in the stack; the matched card does not repeat ever User continues swiping left through remaining cards or taps into another match at any time
    • Stack cycles through the filtered recipe library; when exhausted, should see a “null” screen that also prompts users to adjust filters for more recipes. User should never see a recipe they swiped away in the Discovery Mode (it can still appear in Recipes page though)
    • Saving a recipe from within recipe detail works identically to saving from the Recipes tab -- appears in Saved tab

Flow 6 -- Content Team: Recipe Publishing (Directus)
    • Content team member opens Directus admin dashboard
    • Creates new Recipe record -- fills all fields: title, description, difficulty (1-3), macros, cook time, cube tags, dietary tags
    • Creates RecipeIngredient records and links to recipe
    • Creates RecipeStep records: writes description, uploads MP4 to Cloudinary, pastes Cloudinary URL, sets timer_seconds if step has a timed element, adds tip text if applicable
    • Uploads hero image to Supabase Storage -- links URL to recipe record
    • Saves recipe as draft -- not visible in app or on website
    • Previews recipe via staging URL to QA content and video playback
    • Sets is_published = true -- recipe appears in app library and on supersauced.com immediately
    • Can edit or unpublish recipes (going into edit mode will automatically unpublish recipes). Can republish recipes at any time.

9. Technical Requirements
Final confirmed stack. Shopify is commerce-only. Recipe content and app data live in Supabase. Implementation details and schema migrations live in GitHub.

Area	Detail
Platforms	iOS (App Store, Phase 1 launch priority) and Android (Google Play, to follow shortly after iOS).
Mobile Framework	Expo (React Native) -- managed workflow confirmed. Single codebase for iOS and Android. Cloud builds via Expo EAS (no local Xcode / Android Studio required). Over-the-air JS updates via Expo Updates -- bug fixes ship without App Store review. Expo Router for file-based navigation. NativeWind for styling. TypeScript throughout.
Database & Backend	Supabase -- PostgreSQL database with auto-generated REST and GraphQL API, Supabase Auth, Supabase Storage, and Edge Functions. All recipe content, user data, saves, hearts, and engagement data live here. Row Level Security (RLS) configured from day one. Connection pooling via Supavisor for React Native. TypeScript types auto-generated from schema via Supabase CLI.
Recipe Data Schema	Recipe table: title, slug, description, hero_image_url, servings_default, cook_time_minutes, difficulty (1-3), calories_per_serving, protein_g, fat_g, carbs_g, cube_tags[], dietary_tags[], is_published. RecipeStep table: step_number, description, video_url (Cloudinary MP4), timer_seconds (nullable), tip (nullable). RecipeIngredient table: quantity (decimal), unit, name, notes.
Content Admin	Directus -- open-source headless CMS connected directly to the Supabase PostgreSQL database. Provides the content team a form-based interface for creating, editing, publishing, and managing recipes and steps without engineering involvement. Self-hosted on a $10-20/month Fly.io or DigitalOcean droplet. No separate CMS database -- Directus reads and writes the same Supabase tables the app uses.
Step Videos	Cloudinary -- all Guided Mode step videos hosted and delivered via Cloudinary CDN. Uploaded as MP4 (H.264, optimized for mobile). Cloudinary delivers adaptive quality based on connection speed. Video URLs stored in RecipeStep table in Supabase. Replace-without-URL-change workflow for seamless content updates.
Hero Images	Supabase Storage -- recipe hero images stored and delivered via Supabase CDN. Included in the Supabase plan at no additional cost. Cloudinary reserved for video.
Authentication	Supabase Auth -- Apple Sign In (iOS / Android), Google Sign In (iOS / Android), and email magic link (passwordless). No Shopify SSO dependency at launch. Session tokens stored in platform keychain (iOS) / Keystore (Android). When DTC commerce goes live, a Supabase Edge Function syncs user records to Shopify customer records in the background (Phase 2+).
Data Fetching	TanStack Query (React Query) in the React Native app -- caching (recipe library feels instant on repeat visits), background refetching, and optimistic updates (save / heart actions update UI immediately, sync to Supabase in background).
Deep Linking	Universal Links (iOS) and Android App Links for web-to-app handoff. QR codes on packaging resolve to Next.js recipe landing pages; each page embeds a deep link that routes the user to the correct recipe in-app after install.
Website & QR Landing Pages	Next.js (App Router) on Vercel -- recipe pages use Incremental Static Regeneration (ISR) for fast, cached QR landing pages. JSON-LD Recipe structured data (schema.org) on every page for SEO and Google rich results. Content fetched from Supabase at build / revalidation time. Jump-to-recipe anchor on every page so QR users land on the recipe without scrolling past the app download prompt.
Analytics	PostHog -- product analytics, session replay, and feature flags in one platform. Free tier covers 1M events/month. Feature flags used for phased rollouts and A/B tests without requiring a new app release. See Section 11 for the full tracking plan.
Error Monitoring	Sentry -- mobile crash reporting and error tracking for iOS and Android. GitHub integration surfaces errors directly in the development workflow. Free tier covers early-stage volume; ~$26/month starter plan at scale.
Push Notifications	Phase 2 -- OneSignal. Free up to 10K subscribers. Uses FCM (Android) and APNs (iOS). Visual campaign dashboard lets the marketing team send and schedule pushes without engineering. Supports in-app banners and segmented campaigns.
Commerce	Phase 2+ -- Shopify. DTC product sales, orders, and payments when the product line expands to direct-to-consumer SKUs. Supabase user records synced to Shopify customer records at that point. Shopify has no role in recipe content, authentication, or app data.
Subscription & Payments	Phase 3 -- RevenueCat. In-app subscription and purchase management for iOS and Android. Free tier covers early stage. Add when / if a premium content tier is introduced. Integrates cleanly with Expo and Supabase.
App Store Distribution	Apple App Store and Google Play Store. Builds managed via Expo EAS. App must comply with App Store Review Guidelines and Google Play Developer Policy at all times.
Source Control & Tickets	GitHub -- feature branches, pull requests, and issue tracking. Supabase TypeScript types shared across React Native app, Next.js website, and Edge Functions. Functional requirements and acceptance criteria from this PRD maintained as GitHub Issues.

10. Design Requirements

Visual Direction
The Super Sauced app should feel bold, warm, and unapologetically confident -- a brand that takes great food seriously but never takes itself too seriously. Visual references: Tasty (food-forward photography, high energy), Recess (playful DTC personality, bold color), Fly By Jing (premium product photography, cultural confidence). The result is an app that feels premium but approachable, exciting but not chaotic.
Food photography is the primary design element throughout. Recipes lead with appetite-inspiring hero images. UI chrome supports the content rather than competing with it. Brand personality lives in micro-copy -- empty states, loading messages, the Congrats screen -- not in visual noise.

Typography
App typeface: Rubik (Google Fonts, free). Bold and Black weights carry strong brand personality for headlines; Regular and Medium are clean and readable for body and UI. Works seamlessly across iOS and Android via Expo's font loading.

UX Principles
    • Food first -- the recipe image is always the hero. UI never competes with content.
    • One-handed by default -- every primary action (save, heart, share, next step, timer) is reachable with one thumb in the bottom 60% of the screen.
    • Zero friction paths -- minimize taps between QR scan and recipe, and between recipe and cooking. The core loop (open app, find recipe, start cooking) should feel effortless.
    • Brand in the details -- micro-copy, empty states, the Congrats screen, and loading states are all opportunities to express the Super Sauced voice. These moments should never be generic.
    • Accessible by default -- design for WCAG AA compliance and dynamic type scaling from the very first mockup. Not a retrofit.
    • Confident simplicity -- the app does fewer things than a generic recipe app but does them better. Resist feature creep in the UI; every element on screen should earn its place.

11. Analytics & Tracking Plan
All metrics should be instrumented at or before launch -- retrofitting tracking is costly. Full event schema (user ID, recipe ID, cube tag, session ID, serving size, mode, etc.) to be documented in GitHub before engineering handoff.

Metric	Definition & Formula	Business Signal
Growth & Retention
Monthly Active Users (MAU)	# of unique users who open the app at least once in a rolling 30-day window	Baseline engagement health; track the trend and delta, not the absolute number
New Users	# of users completing account creation for the first time in the period	Acquisition rate; measures how well the QR-to-download funnel converts
Returning Users	# of users who were active last period AND active this period	Core retained base; the healthiest growth signal
Churned Users	# of users active last period but NOT active this period	Early warning; spikes may signal content staleness or UX friction
Net User Growth	New Users - Churned Users (per period)	True growth signal -- strips the vanity out of raw MAU
Day 7 Retention Rate	% of new users who return on or after Day 7 -- # returning Day 7 / # in acquisition cohort	Strongest early signal of whether the product has a genuine hook
Day 30 Retention Rate	% of new users still active 30 days after first open -- # active Day 30 / # in acquisition cohort	Long-term stickiness; benchmark for content-driven apps is 20-30%
Onboarding
Onboarding Completion Rate	% of users who finish account setup after first app open -- # completed onboarding / # first opens	Friction indicator; drop-off here means losing users before they see any value
Onboarding Drop-off by Step	% of users who exit at each onboarding step -- # who exited at step N / # who entered step N	Pinpoints exactly where setup breaks down; informs UX iteration priority
QR-to-Download Conversion Rate	% of Shopify recipe page visitors (from QR scan) who tap the app download link -- # taps / # QR-originated page views	Measures how well the web-to-app handoff is working
Recipe Engagement
Recipe Views	# of recipe detail page opens in the period (total and per recipe)	Baseline content demand; which recipes attract the most attention
Save Rate	% of recipe views that result in a save -- # saves / # views (per recipe and overall)	Intent signal; a save means the user wants to cook this
Heart Rate	% of recipe views that result in a heart -- # hearts / # views (per recipe and overall)	Popularity signal; drives content ranking and future curation decisions
Share Rate	% of recipe views that result in a share -- # shares / # views (per recipe and overall)	Organic growth signal; high share rate = word-of-mouth potential
Guided Mode Completion Rate	% of Guided Mode sessions where the user reaches the final step -- # sessions at final step / # Guided Mode sessions started	Tells us whether users are actually cooking, not just browsing
Saves per Active User (monthly)	Total saves in period / MAU	Depth of engagement; rising number means content is resonating
Hearts per Active User (monthly)	Total hearts in period / MAU	Affinity metric; complements saves to show emotional connection with content
Content Mode Preference
Guided Mode Usage Rate	% of recipe opens that start a Guided Mode session -- # Guided Mode starts / # recipe detail opens	Tells us how many users are cooking vs. browsing; informs GIF content investment
Quick View Usage Rate	% of recipe opens that use Quick View -- # Quick View opens / # recipe detail opens

(user stays on longer than 1min)	Signals experienced cooks; high Quick View share = confident, returning user base
Mode Switch Rate	% of recipe sessions where the user toggles between modes -- # sessions with a mode switch / # total recipe sessions	UX signal; frequent switching may indicate friction in one mode
Serving Size
Mean Serving Size	Average serving size selected across all sessions with an adjustment -- sum of selected serving sizes / # sessions where size was changed	Household size proxy; tells us the average group our users are cooking for
Mode Serving Size	Most frequently selected serving size across all adjustment sessions	Single most common household type; informs recipe defaults and content framing
Serving Size Distribution	% of adjustment sessions by size -- # sessions at size N / # total adjustment sessions; reported for 1, 2, 4, 6, 8+	Full household composition picture; guides portion guidance and recipe scaling
Top 5% User Serving Habits	Mean and mode serving size calculated within the top 5% of users by session count	Power user household proxy; most engaged cooks inform premium content and loyalty strategy
Search & Discovery
Top Search Terms	Ranked list of search queries by # of times entered in the period	Content gap finder; high-volume terms with thin results = recipes to prioritize
Zero-Result Search Rate	% of searches returning no results -- # zero-result searches / # total searches	Direct measure of content gaps; should trend toward 0 as library grows
Filter Usage Rate	% of sessions that include at least one filter selection -- # sessions with any filter / # total sessions	Tells us how actively users are using discovery tools vs. pure scrolling
Top Filters Used	Ranked count of each filter selection (cube/SKU, cook time, dietary tag) -- # times each filter applied in the period	Taste and lifestyle trend data; directly informs product development and SKU strategy
SKU & Cube Performance
Cube Filter Selection Count	# of times each cube/SKU filter is selected in the period, reported per SKU	Reveals which cubes drive the most recipe interest; feeds inventory and marketing decisions
Save Rate by Cube	% of views for recipes tagged with a given cube that result in a save -- # saves / # views, per cube tag	Content quality signal per SKU; low save rate = recipe content needs improvement
Heart Rate by Cube	% of views for recipes tagged with a given cube that result in a heart -- # hearts / # views, per cube tag	Fan affinity per SKU; which cubes have the strongest emotional pull
Behavioral Signals
Peak Usage Hours	# of app sessions per hour bucket (e.g. 6-9am, 12-1pm, 5-8pm) across all users in the period	Reveals when customers are meal planning and cooking; informs push notification timing (Phase 2)
Session Frequency	Avg # of sessions per active user per month -- total sessions / MAU	Habit formation indicator; rising frequency = the app is becoming a kitchen staple
Onboarding Survey
Survey Completion Rate	% of new users who answer at least one survey question -- # users who answered >= 1 question / # users who saw the survey	Measures whether the survey creates friction; target is high completion given questions are skippable
Skip Rate per Question	% of users who skip each individual question -- # skips on question N / # users who reached question N	Shows which questions feel too personal or confusing; informs future survey design
Cube Purchase Distribution	% breakdown of reported cube purchases across all SKUs -- # users who selected cube X / # users who answered Q1	First-party purchase signal; validates retail sell-through data and informs content prioritization
Dietary Preference Distribution	% breakdown of dietary preference selections across all tags -- # users who selected tag X / # users who answered Q2	Audience lifestyle profile at sign-up; seeds personalization and informs filter content investment
Brand Discovery Channel Mix	% breakdown of how users report discovering Super Sauced -- # users selecting each option / # users who answered Q3	Marketing attribution signal; shows which acquisition channels are driving the most downloads
Post-Cook Rating & Comments (Phase 1 -- display in Phase 2)
Congrats Screen Impression Rate	% of Guided Mode completions that result in the Congrats screen being shown -- # Congrats screens shown / # Guided Mode sessions reaching final step	Measures how many users are actually finishing recipes end-to-end
Rating Submission Rate	% of Congrats screen impressions that result in a thumbs up or down rating -- # ratings submitted / # Congrats screens shown	Engagement with the rating feature; low rate = screen is being dismissed; high rate = users want to give feedback
Heart	% of submitted ratings that give hearts to recipe -- # hearts / # total ratings submitted	Post-cook satisfaction score per recipe; distinct from hearts (pre-cook intent) -- high hearts + low thumbs up = appealing but hard to execute
“X” rate	% of submitted ratings that are “X”ed out -- # “X” ratings / # total ratings submitted	Execution failure signal; spikes on specific recipes indicate unclear instructions or ingredient issues
Comment Submission Rate	% of ratings that include a written comment -- # comments submitted / # ratings submitted	Depth of post-cook engagement; comments feed qualitative content improvements and future community feed
Discovery Mode (Phase 2)
Discovery Mode Session Rate	% of active users who open the Discover tab at least once in the period -- # users with >= 1 Discover session / MAU	Adoption signal; tracks whether users find and return to the feature
Match Rate	% of cards shown that result in a tap (match) -- # taps / # total cards shown	Affinity signal; rising rate means the card content is compelling enough to open
Skip Rate	% of cards shown that result in a left swipe -- # swipe lefts / # total cards shown	Rejection signal; patterns in skipped cards reveal which recipe types consistently fail to connect
Discovery-to-Save Rate	% of matched (tapped) recipes that are subsequently saved -- # saves initiated from Discover / # matches	Intent quality; high match rate with low save rate = users are curious but not committing
Discovery-to-Cook Rate	% of matched recipes that result in a Guided Mode session -- # Guided Mode starts from Discover / # matches	Conversion quality; the strongest signal that Discovery Mode is driving actual cooking behavior
Top Skipped Recipe Tags	Ranked list of cube/SKU and dietary tags on recipes with the highest skip rates in the period	Content signal; consistently skipped tags inform which recipe types to reduce or reframe in the Discover stack
CRM & Demographics  (collected via account creation + future rewards program)
Age Distribution	% of registered users by age bracket (18-24, 25-34, 35-44, 45-54, 55+) -- # users in bracket / total registered users with age on file	Audience composition; informs tone, content focus, and marketing targeting
Birthday Data Coverage	% of registered users with a birthday on file -- # users with birthday / total registered users	Enables birthday promos; tracks progress toward CRM completeness
Retail Location Data	# of users who have indicated a preferred retailer (surfaced via geo-targeted promos)	Feeds retailer partnership conversations and regional inventory signals

12. Risks & Assumptions
R = Risk (something that could go wrong). A = Assumption (something we are treating as true). Revisit this list at the start of each sprint.

	Type	Risk / Assumption	Likelihood	Mitigation
A	Assumption	Users who scan the QR code on packaging are motivated enough to download the app after landing on the Shopify recipe page	Medium	Optimize the web-to-app handoff page (prominent download prompt, clear value proposition above the fold). Measure QR-to-download conversion from day one and iterate on page copy and layout.
A	Assumption	Apple Sign In and Google Sign In meaningfully reduce sign-up friction compared to email-only registration	Low	Confirmed best practice for consumer mobile apps. Monitor onboarding completion rate and drop-off by auth method post-launch.
A	Assumption	A recipe library of [TBD] recipes at launch is sufficient to provide enough variety to retain users through the first 30 days	Medium	Define a minimum recipe count before launch begins. Prioritize content diversity across all cubes, dietary tags, and cook time buckets rather than raw quantity.
R	Risk	Supabase + Directus integration introduces unexpected complexity or instability at production scale	Low	Both are well-documented, actively maintained open-source tools with official integration support. Run a technical spike in the first sprint to validate the connection. Self-host Directus on a reliable cloud provider.
R	Risk	Cloudinary step video delivery is slow on poor mobile connections, breaking the Guided Mode experience	Medium	Use Cloudinary's adaptive quality delivery. Preload the next step's video while the user is on the current step. Define a maximum video file size and enforce it in the content team workflow.
R	Risk	Expo managed workflow hits a native capability ceiling for a feature added in Phase 2 or 3	Low	Expo's managed workflow supports the vast majority of consumer app features. If a custom native module is ever needed, eject to bare workflow via npx expo prebuild -- a non-destructive migration.
R	Risk	Supabase Row Level Security (RLS) is misconfigured at launch, exposing recipe or user data incorrectly	Medium	Configure and test RLS policies as the first infrastructure task before any data is written. Include RLS policy review in the engineering definition of done for every data-touching feature.
R	Risk	App Store review rejection delays Phase 1 launch -- Apple is strict about health, food, and data collection	Medium	Review App Store guidelines before submission, particularly around health/nutrition claims and dietary data collection. Allow 2-3 weeks of buffer in the launch timeline for review cycles.
R	Risk	Low organic retention after initial novelty -- users download, browse once, and do not return	High	Design return habits into the product: More Recipes You'll Love, post-cook ratings, and saved recipes all create reasons to return. Plan Phase 2 push notifications proactively. Monitor Day 7 and Day 30 retention as the leading indicators.
R	Risk	Recipe content quality or quantity is insufficient to sustain engagement, causing users to exhaust the library quickly	Medium	Set a minimum content cadence (e.g. 2 new recipes per week post-launch). Track recipe view diversity -- if users are repeatedly viewing the same small set of recipes, it signals a content gap.
R	Risk	Discovery Mode (Phase 2) drives high match rates but low cook rates -- users swipe for entertainment, not to cook	Medium	Track match-to-cook conversion from day one after launch. If the rate is low, introduce a friction point (e.g. a prompt after 10 left-swipes: 'Ready to pick one?') to drive decision rather than endless browsing.
A	Assumption	The internal content team can manage recipe creation, video upload, and publishing in Directus without ongoing engineering support after initial onboarding	Medium	Run a content team training session before launch. Document the full publishing workflow. Build a simple content QA checklist. If Directus proves too complex, evaluate a lightweight custom admin form.
A	Assumption	Each Super Sauced cube SKU has enough distinct recipe use cases to justify its own filter at launch	Low	Confirm minimum recipe count per cube before enabling its filter. Do not surface a cube filter if fewer than 3 recipes are tagged with it at launch.
A	Assumption	The app drives measurable repeat purchase behavior that can be attributed to app engagement	Medium	Instrument QR scan source tracking and coupon/promo code attribution in Phase 2. Design at least one in-app prompt that connects recipe completion to a retail purchase call to action.
